The Big Bore Supermoto Adventure

As a beginning rider, aside from the motorcycle skills course, I had very little exposure to other teachers before my first few accidents. Like many other beginners I saw the effect of many years of practice and I immediately wanted to reproduce it in myself. It was a recipe for learning the hard way.

Despite my early failures I love riding. One of the best things I have done for myself since is to enroll in motorcycle riding classes, create opportunities for low speed control drills, and find ways to explore my limits with any eye on limiting my “push to perform” to 70% much of the time.

One of the techniques that I was missing early on and have only recently started to understand is the concept of counter-weighting the pegs for cornering.

In low speed drills involving tight turning radiuses this is invaluable - it is also a safe place to explore the concept.

The concept is essentially one of rider engagement. Treating the bike as an extension of one’s body instead of simply being a passenger on your own ride.

By engaging the hip and leg muscles and balancing your weight into the outside peg for the corner - you are effectively evening out the load across the contact patch of the rear tire.

This is something that can be explored with many riding styles - be it on a sport bike when one is leaning inside the bike to lower the center of gravity and require less lean angle or on a supermoto where one is attacking an apex on the outside edge of the seat - in fact in both situations the counter-weight of the outside peg becomes more and more essential as confidence develops.

Remember - Ride safe - Ride prepared and Keep developing control.
